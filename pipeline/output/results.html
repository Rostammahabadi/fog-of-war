Methodology: Based on the paper "When AI Navigates the Fog of War" (Li et al., 2026). Each model receives temporally-gated intelligence briefings (2,500 news articles from 11 outlets, capped at ~480K characters per node) and independently answers 42 verifiable binary questions across 11 nodes, matching the paper's single-question protocol (no system prompt, temperature 0.3, max 2048 tokens). 1-MAE (Calibration Consistency) is the paper's primary metric: 1 minus mean absolute error between predicted probability and binary outcome (higher = better). Brier Score measures calibration (lower = better). Accuracy is binary prediction correctness (p > 0.5).

Key difference from paper: The paper used manual human probability extraction by two researchers. This pipeline uses automated LLM-based extraction (Claude Haiku 4.5), which adds extraction noise and likely explains the ~0.05 lower scores for most models. Thematic patterns are highly consistent with the paper's findings: Economic Shockwaves is the strongest theme (our 0.790 vs paper 0.79), Initial Outbreak closely matches (0.743 vs 0.74), while Political Signaling shows the largest divergence (0.529 vs 0.67). Claude Opus 4.6 is our addition (not evaluated in the original paper) and achieves the highest overall 1-MAE of 0.776.
